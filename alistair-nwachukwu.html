Meet Alistair Nwachukwu, Who Played Adrik in “Shadow and Bone”

Alistair Nwachukwu (age: 27 years: born in 1995) is a budding British actor from Rochester, England, who made it into the headlines after his sublime performance in season 2 of “Shadow and Bone” which came out on March 16, 2023.

In this long-awaited continuation of the famous Netflix show, Nwachukwu played the role of Joanna McGibbon’s on-screen brother Adrik.

FYI, “Shadow and Bone” is Alistair’s first prominent project; in the past, he was involved majorly in stage shows and has only worked on two short movies, including “The Mourning Bird” and “Sophie and the Danegeld.”

Nwachukwu is a living embodiment for those who believe in extraordinary dreams and work day and night to fulfill those dreams. If you want to know how Alistair became a TV celebrity as a stage artist, then keep reading.

How Old is Alistair Nwachukwu?

In 1995, Alistair Nwachukwu (age 27) was born in an Anglo-African family in Rochester, England, United Kingdom. He was very little when the dream of becoming an actor culminated in his mind; maybe his love for action movies is why he chose to become an actor.

While studying at the Thomas Aveling School, he participated in numerous stage shows and continued doing so until his high school graduation. In 2017 he did a one-year LAMDA Foundation Diploma, which gave him a chance to explore his interests and understand what he wants to excel at.

The following year he got accepted to earn BA-Professional Acting; meanwhile, he also received a three-year-long Audible Scholarship.

Alistair Nwachukwu’s Acting Career

While at the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art, Alistair worked extensively as a stage artist. In three years, he worked on 7 popular theatre productions, including “Hamilton,” “Father and Sons,” “Boy,” “Red Velvet,” “A Doll’s House” and “Spalding Suite.” In the meantime, he gave audition coaching to aspiring actors to earn some money and fuel his career.

In 2022, Suzanne Smith and Sophie Holland recognized Nwachukwu’s talent and offered him the role of Adrik; Alistair understood the importance of the opportunity and gave his best performance.

The series landed on Netflix a year later, and Al won viewers’ hearts. The audience loved his performance during the fighting scenes, and critics specifically mentioned this Rochester native’s contribution to the success of the series.
