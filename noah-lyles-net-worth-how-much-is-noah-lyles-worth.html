Noah Lyles Net Worth: How Much is Noah Lyles Worth? – Noah Lyles is a distinguished American professional sprinter specializing in both the 100 meters as well as 200 meters.

Born on July 18, 1997, his impressive track and field career includes several remarkable achievements, including a bronze medal in the 200 meters at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics. Lyles is a three-time World champion, having secured victory in the 200 meters at both the 2019 and 2022 World Championships and the 100 meters at the 2023 World Championships.

At the 2022 Championships, he also contributed to his team’s success by earning gold and silver medals in the 4 × 100 meters relay events. Lyles boasts exceptional personal best times of 9.83 seconds in the 100 meters and 19.31 seconds in the 200 meters, the latter being an American record that places him as the third-fastest sprinter on the world all-time list in the 200 meters.

Lyles’ journey to success began with a gold medal in the 200 meters at the 2014 Youth Olympic Games. He continued to shine on the international stage, winning gold medals in the 100 meters and the 4 × 100 meters relay at the 2016 World U20 Championships. Additionally, he is a five-time Diamond League champion, achieving the rare feat of securing the 100 meters/200 meters double in 2019.

In his early years, Noah Lyles displayed his athletic talent as a gymnast and transitioned to track and field at the age of 12.

In 2019, Lyles opened the season with impressive performances, setting a world-leading time of 9.86 seconds in the 100 meters in Shanghai on May 18. He also made history by equaling Pietro Mennea’s long-standing world record time of 19.72 seconds in the 200 meters at the Pietro Mennea Golden Gala meet.

The extended schedule in 2019 allowed him to participate in the US National Championships, where he secured the 200-meter title with a time of 19.78 seconds, despite challenging headwinds in Des Moines. He continued to impress with a time of 19.65 seconds in Paris.

Lyles’ outstanding performances culminated in him winning gold medals in both the 200 meters and the 4 × 100 meters relay at the 2019 World Athletics Championships in Doha, Qatar.

In 2021, at the delayed 2020 Tokyo Olympics, Lyles added a bronze medal in the 200 meters to his impressive collection, achieving a time of 19.74 seconds. He followed this up with another remarkable performance, running 19.52 seconds in Eugene, Oregon, on August 21, marking the ninth-fastest 200-meter time in history.

On July 21, 2022, during the World Athletics Championships held in Eugene, Oregon, Lyles achieved another milestone in his career by securing his second global title in the 200 meters. Notably, he surpassed Michael Johnson’s longstanding record of 19.32 seconds by clocking an impressive time of 19.31 seconds, placing him third on the world all-time list in the 200 meters.

Lyles concluded his exceptional season by clinching victory in the 200 meters at the Zürich Diamond League final, earning his fourth Diamond Trophy in this distance and fifth overall. Throughout the year, Lyles remained undefeated in his specialist event, consistently breaking the 20-second barrier in all 12 of his races, including heats and finals.
